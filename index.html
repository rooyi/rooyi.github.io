Renn Su

Palo Alto, CA

I'm a human-computer interaction researcher and creative technologist. Currently, I study computer science at Stanford University as a coterminal master’s student, focusing on artificial intelligence and human-computer interaction. I’m advised by Prof. Michael Bernstein as part of the Stanford HCI Group, where my research interests include generative agents, multi-agent systems, and LLM value elicitation. This summer, I'm working at Mila and Montréal HCI, where I'm supervised by Prof. Ian Arawjo.

Before that, I completed my B.S. in computer science at Stanford with honors. My thesis received a Firestone Medal for Excellence in Undergraduate Research (one of two awarded in the computer science department). I've also worked with the PEACH Lab at ETH Zürich as a summer research fellow, hosted by Prof. April Wang.
